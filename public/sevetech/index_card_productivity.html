Foundation

Effective Note-Taking Starts Here

An index card, also known as a cue card or flash card in some regions, is a small piece of rigid paper, typically measuring 3 by 5 inches 7.6 by 12.7 cm) or 4 by 6 inches 10.2 by 15.2 cm). The cards are usually blank on one side, allowing users to write or print information, while the other side may be lined or blank, depending on the design.
Double-sided ruled index cards are a variation of standard index cards that feature lines on both sides of the card. This design allows for more writing space and organization, making them particularly useful for various applications. With lines on both sides, users can take advantage of the full card for notes, making them ideal for detailed study notes, lists, or outlines.
Strategic Colour Categorisation
Assigning coloured tabs to specific subjects reduces cognitive load and dramatically speeds up retrieval. Your eye finds the right category before your brain has to think.

Evolution

The Notebook Evolution

While traditional index cards offer flexibility, they're prone to scattering and disorganization without a structured system. An alternative approach involves using a notebook with printed index card pages. This method combines the benefits of index cards with the stability of a notebook, creating a system that prevents cards from getting lost, misplaced, or arranged haphazardly—while maintaining the organizational freedom that makes index cards effective.

Why constrained space sharpens AI prompts
A card's limited space forces you to distil instructions to their essence. What fits on a card is almost always a better prompt than a sprawling paragraph — the physical constraint is a thinking tool.
